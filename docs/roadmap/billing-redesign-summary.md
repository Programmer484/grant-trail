# Billing Redesign — Owner Summary

## What we're proposing to change

**Today:** everyone pays individually. A fiscal agent pays for their plan, and each grantee they bring on also needs their own subscription.

**Proposed:** the fiscal agent's plan covers their entire organization. One payment, everyone under that agent is included automatically.

---

## Three user types instead of two

| Who | Today | Proposed |
|---|---|---|
| **Fiscal agent** | Pays premium | Same — pays premium, covers their whole org |
| **Grantee** | Pays their own basic subscription | Free if under a fiscal agent's org |
| **Seeker** (charity looking for a sponsor) | Pays basic to browse the directory | **Free** — just needs an account |

---

## Why make seeking free?

Right now we charge seekers to browse and contact fiscal agents. The problem: a seeker's whole goal is to find a sponsor and stop paying. Charging them to do that creates friction on the side of the platform we want to grow.

Making seeking free means more charities in the pool, which makes the directory more valuable to fiscal agents — the side that actually pays.

---

## What stays the same

- Fiscal agents still pay premium to list and manage their organization.
- Self-service grantees (no sponsor) still pay basic for the grant tracking app.
- All existing grant tracking, approvals, budgets, and reporting are unchanged.

---

## Current state without this change

The platform works today. The main trade-off is a small manual step required each time a new fiscal agent onboards their team. At low volume this is manageable. The redesign eliminates it and sets up the directory for growth.
